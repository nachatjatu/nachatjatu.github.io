about me

I am finishing up my M.S. in Management Science and Engineering (MS&E) at Stanford, where I also completed my B.S. in Computer Science in 2025.

My research interests lie broadly in the decision sciences, where I hope to study how people respond to incentives and uncertainty in socioeconomic systems. I am inspired by ideas from dynamic optimization, mechanism design, and machine learning. I aim to use tools from these domains to understand such interactions in operations settings such as supply chains, services, and matching platforms.

Some themes that excite me include sustainability, emerging economies, and public-private interfaces.
